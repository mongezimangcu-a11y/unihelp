# Can NSFAS pay for private accommodation?

Many students ask this question when they do not get a place in residence: **can NSFAS pay for private accommodation?**

The short answer is **yes, in some cases**, but it depends on the rules that apply to your institution, your accommodation, and whether that accommodation is approved.

## NSFAS does not automatically pay every private landlord

It is important to understand that NSFAS does **not** simply pay for any private room or flat a student chooses.

Private accommodation usually has to meet certain requirements before it can be recognised for student funding support.

This means students should not assume that every off-campus option will automatically be covered.

## When can private accommodation be considered?

Private accommodation may be considered when:

- you are funded by NSFAS
- you are not placed in university residence
- your institution recognises approved private accommodation options
- the accommodation meets the required standards
- the accommodation process is handled according to institutional and funding rules

In other words, private accommodation support usually depends on whether the place is recognised or approved within the system that applies to your institution.

## Why approval matters

Approval matters because student accommodation is not only about having a place to stay. It is also about:

- safety
- distance from campus
- suitable living conditions
- proper student support standards
- cost limits

This is why institutions often work with approved accommodation providers rather than allowing any private arrangement to count.

## Can you choose any private place and expect NSFAS to pay?

Usually, no.

If you move into accommodation that is not recognised or not properly approved, you may face problems such as:

- no accommodation support being processed
- delays
- disputes with landlords
- confusion about payment
- financial pressure on you or your family

This is why students should always check first before signing anything.

## What should you do before moving in?

Before committing to private accommodation, it is wise to:

- ask your institution what accommodation rules apply
- check whether the accommodation is recognised or approved
- understand what documents may be needed
- confirm what support is actually included
- avoid relying only on verbal promises

This can save you from serious problems later.

## Does NSFAS pay the student directly?

The way accommodation support is handled can vary depending on the institution and the payment arrangements in place.

That is why students should not guess. They should always confirm with the institution or the official funding process used there.

## What if you already found a place?

If you already found private accommodation, do not panic. But do not assume it will be covered either.

You should quickly confirm:

- whether the accommodation is recognised
- whether the cost is acceptable under the rules
- whether your institution allows it
- what steps you must follow next

It is always better to check early than to discover later that the accommodation is not supported.

## Final advice

NSFAS may support private accommodation in some cases, but students should never assume that any private place will automatically be paid for.

The safest approach is to:
- check the rules at your institution
- confirm approval first
- avoid signing blindly
- keep records of all important communication
