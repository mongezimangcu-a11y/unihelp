# How do APS points work?

APS (Admission Point Score) is used by South African universities to decide
whether you qualify for certain programmes.

Want to calculate yours right now?  
**Try the [APS Calculator](/tools/aps-calculator).**

---

## How APS points are calculated

Most universities convert your subject percentages into points using a table similar to this:

- 80–100% → 7 points  
- 70–79% → 6 points  
- 60–69% → 5 points  
- 50–59% → 4 points  
- 40–49% → 3 points  
- 30–39% → 2 points  
- Below 30% → 1 point  

These points are then added together to give your total APS.

---

## How many subjects are counted?

In most cases:
- Universities count your **best 6 subjects**
- **Life Orientation is often excluded**
- Some universities may count **7 subjects**, depending on the programme

To see the difference between counting best 6 vs all 7 (and including/excluding LO), use the  
**[APS Calculator](/tools/aps-calculator)** — it lets you toggle these rules instantly.

---

## Important subject requirements

APS alone is not enough.

Some degrees also require specific subjects, for example:
- Engineering usually requires **Mathematics and Physical Sciences**
- Medicine usually requires **Mathematics, Physical Sciences, and Life Sciences**
- Commerce often requires **Mathematics**
- Law and Humanities usually have **no specific subject requirements**
