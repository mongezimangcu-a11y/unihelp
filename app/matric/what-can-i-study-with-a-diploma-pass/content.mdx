# What can I study with a Diploma pass? (South Africa)

A **Diploma pass** is a Matric pass level that can allow you to apply for **diplomas** at universities of technology and some universities — but admission depends on your **APS** and specific programme requirements.

---

## Quick facts (so you understand it fast)

With a Diploma pass, you may be able to apply for:
- Diplomas (often 3 years) at universities of technology
- Some university diplomas (depending on the institution)
- Higher certificates (usually easier entry requirements)
- Some bridging or extended programmes (where offered)

> A Diploma pass does **not** automatically guarantee a place — programmes can be competitive.

---

## Minimum requirements for a Diploma pass

In general, a Diploma pass requires:

- **Home Language:** 40% or more  
- **Three other subjects (excluding LO):** 40% or more  
- **Two other subjects:** 30% or more  
- You must pass at least **6 of 7 subjects**

Universities also check your **APS** and required subjects.

## Examples of diploma fields you can study

Here are common diploma areas (examples):

### Business & Commerce
- Financial Accounting
- Human Resource Management
- Marketing
- Management / Business Management
- Public Management

### IT & Tech
- Information Technology
- Software Development (some institutions)
- Networking / Systems Support

### Engineering (often requires Maths + Physical Sciences)
- Electrical Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Civil Engineering

> Many engineering diplomas require **Mathematics** (not Math Lit) and **Physical Sciences**.

### Health & Community Services
- Environmental Health (varies)
- Emergency Medical Care pathways (often strict requirements)
- Public health-related diplomas (varies)

### Hospitality & Tourism
- Hospitality Management
- Tourism Management

### Creative / Design
- Graphic Design
- Fashion Design (where offered)
- Multimedia / Digital design (varies)

---

## The most important thing: APS + required subjects

Two students can both have a Diploma pass, but only one qualifies for a specific programme because of:
- APS score
- Maths vs Maths Lit
- Physical Sciences / Life Sciences requirements
- Language requirements

## What if your APS is too low?

If you don’t qualify for the diploma you want, consider:
- A **Higher Certificate** first, then progress to a Diploma (where pathways exist)
- Rewriting specific subjects to improve APS
- TVET college programmes (great practical routes)
