# Can you rewrite Matric in South Africa? (Yes — here’s how)

Yes. You can rewrite Matric to improve your results or qualify for a different pass level (Bachelor/Diploma), but the best option depends on your age, your previous results, and what you’re trying to achieve.

---

## Quick reasons students rewrite Matric

Students commonly rewrite to:
- Increase APS for a competitive programme
- Improve Mathematics or Physical Sciences marks
- Move from a Diploma pass to a Bachelor pass
- Fix one or two subjects that pulled their results down
- Meet subject requirements (Maths vs Math Lit matters)

---

## Option 1: Rewrite specific subjects (most common)

This is usually the smartest option if only a few subjects need improvement.

### How it works
- You choose the subject(s) you want to rewrite
- You study again and write the exams
- Your improved subject results can increase your APS

---

## Option 2: Rewrite the full Matric

This is usually only needed if your overall results were poor or you want to change many subjects.

### How it works
- You register for all subjects again
- You rewrite the full set of exams

This takes longer and is more work than rewriting a few subjects.

---

## Option 3: Second Chance / supplementary support programmes

Some students use structured programmes (often called “second chance” support) to prepare for rewrites.

This helps if you need:
- better study structure
- better support for Maths/Science
- exam practice and time management

---

## Option 4: Adult Matric / upgrading routes (depending on your situation)

Some upgrading pathways are designed for adults or learners who completed school years ago.

These routes can help you:
- improve specific subjects
- complete missing requirements
- reach diploma/degree entry where possible

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Rewriting without a clear target (what programme are you aiming for?)
- Ignoring subject requirements (some degrees need **Maths**, not Math Lit)
- Not calculating APS before and after rewriting
- Trying to “rewrite everything” when only 1–2 subjects need improvement

---

## Best next step: calculate your APS first

Before you rewrite anything, calculate your APS now and decide what you need to improve.

Try the **[APS Calculator](/tools/aps-calculator)**:
- count best 6 vs 7
- include/exclude LO
- check Maths vs Math Lit warning for certain degrees

---

## How to choose which subjects to rewrite

A smart approach is:
1. Identify your target degree/diploma
2. Check required subjects (Maths/Science/languages)
3. Rewrite the subject(s) that:
   - block you from entry requirements, or
   - will give you the biggest APS increase

Often, improving one subject from a low band to a higher band can increase your APS quickly.
