# What happens if you fail Matric?

Failing Matric can feel like the end — but it isn’t. You still have options,
and many students take an alternative route and succeed.

---

## Step 1: Understand what “fail” means

You might have:
- Failed one or more subjects, but still qualified for a pass level
- Missed the requirements for a Bachelor/Diploma pass
- Failed enough subjects that you didn’t get a pass level

Your next step depends on your results and what you want to study.

---

## Option 1: Rewrite Matric

You can rewrite:
- Specific subjects you did poorly in
- Or the full Matric (less common)

This can improve your marks and increase your APS.

---

## Option 2: Upgrade your results

Upgrading usually means improving certain subjects through:
- Second Chance programmes
- Adult education centres / colleges
- Private institutions that help you prepare

---

## Option 3: Do a higher certificate or diploma pathway

Many students take a route like:
- Higher Certificate → Diploma → Degree (depending on institution and performance)

This can be a strong option if you didn’t meet degree entry requirements.

---

## Option 4: Explore TVET and skills programmes

TVET colleges offer practical programmes that can lead to real careers.
This can be a great path if you want hands-on training.

---

## Next step: calculate your APS

Even if you rewrite or upgrade, APS matters.  
Try the **[APS Calculator](/tools/aps-calculator)** to see where you stand and what you need to improve.
