# When do bursary applications open in South Africa?

One of the most common questions students ask is: **when do bursary applications open?**

The answer depends on the bursary provider. Unlike NSFAS, bursaries do not open through one single national system. Different organisations open and close applications at different times.

That is why students should prepare early and check official bursary pages regularly.

## Bursary dates are not all the same

Each bursary provider decides its own:

- opening date
- closing date
- required documents
- application process
- eligibility rules

This means one bursary may open much earlier than another, even if both support the same type of student.

## When do bursary applications usually open?

Many bursary applications open during the year before the study period they are funding.

This is why students should start looking early, especially if they are in Grade 12 or preparing for first-year studies.

Some bursaries open:
- early in the year
- mid-year
- towards the end of the year

There is no single bursary season that applies to all providers.

## Why students miss bursary deadlines

Many students miss bursary deadlines because they:

- only start searching too late
- assume bursaries open at the same time as NSFAS
- do not prepare documents early
- do not check official websites regularly
- wait until the closing day
- rely on rumours or outdated social media posts

A missed deadline can mean waiting many more months for the next opportunity.

## What should you do before bursaries open?

Even if you do not know the exact opening date yet, you can still prepare.

Start by getting these ready:

- South African ID
- latest academic results
- proof of acceptance or application where relevant
- proof of household income if needed
- CV
- motivation letter
- proof of residence if required

This will help you apply faster when a bursary opens.

## Where should you check bursary dates?

The safest place to check is always the **official bursary provider website** or official announcement page.

Do not rely only on:
- forwarded WhatsApp messages
- random social media graphics
- unofficial websites with no source

Use trusted sources and confirm deadlines directly.

## Should you wait until you get final results?

Not always.

Some bursaries accept:
- Grade 11 results
- latest available academic results
- currently enrolled student results

Others may require final matric results or proof of registration.

So it is important to read each bursary carefully instead of assuming they all work the same way.

## Final advice

Bursary application dates vary, so the best strategy is to stay prepared all year.

Do this:

- check official bursary sites regularly
- prepare documents early
- track closing dates
- apply before the last day
- keep copies of everything you submit

Students who are organised usually have a much better chance of applying successfully.
